Project: Fan Engagement & Ticketing Bot Analysis

Problem Overview

Automated bots and scalpers reduce real fans' ability to buy tickets at face value, inflate secondary market prices, and damage brand trust. Key risks include:

  • Denial of inventory: Bots add tickets to carts but never check out, preventing real customers from purchasing.
  • Server overload: Automated traffic overwhelms servers, causing slow performance or crashes.
  • Card fraud: Bots test stolen or cracked payment cards.
  • Scraping: Bots steal listings and content, posting them to competitor sites, losing visibility and revenue.

Example: Ticket scalping technology and bot usage can lead to up to 60% of tickets being purchased by bots (Museums and the Web, 2017). Only 46% of tickets are typically available to the general public (NY Attorney General).

Solutions & Recommendations

1. Use Effective CAPTCHA

  • Integrate multi-signal CAPTCHAs to detect sophisticated bots.
  • Continuously improve using real challenge data.
  • Connect CAPTCHAs to anti-bot platforms for full protection.

2. Establish Smart Buying Limits

  • Limit tickets per transaction, session, or day (e.g., 4–8 tickets).
  • Provide verified exceptions for corporate groups or fan clubs.
  • Monitor for fake accounts attempting to bypass limits.

3. Invest in Anti-Bot Technology

  • Analyze every request, not just samples.
  • Maintain low false positive rates.
  • Continuously evolve detection to match bot sophistication.
